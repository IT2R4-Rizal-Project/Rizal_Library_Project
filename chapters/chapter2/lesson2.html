Lesson 2: Rizal's Life: Higher Education and Life Overseas

Early Education

José had a sharp mind from a young age. When he was three years old, his mother taught him the alphabet, and by the time he was five, he could read and write.

Rizal first studied under Justiniano Aquino Cruz in Biñan, before he was sent to Manila.

Paciano traveled to Biñan, Laguna, with his younger brother, Jose, in 1870 to help him enroll at Sr. Cruz, Justiniano Aquino. Because Jose would already be beginning his secondary school at Ateneo Municipal de Manila, he also accompanied him to Manila two years later.

Education at Ateneo (1872-1877)

Rizal took first the entrance examination at the Colegio de San Juan de Letran on June 10, 1872. During that time, the Dominicans had the authority to examine and regulate Ateneo, therefore the exams for new freshmen in the several colleges for boys were held at Colegio de San Juan de Letran. Rizal took the test and was accepted to the Ateneo Municipal.

Father Magin Ferrand (Fernando), who was the college registrar at the time, denied Rizal admission despite the fact that he passed the entrance exam for two reasons:

  • 1st: He was a late registrant
  • 2nd: He was extremely thin, pale, and undersized for an 11-year-old.

At Ateneo, students were introduced to the Jesuit System of Education, which emphasized discipline and encouraged healthy competition among learners.

  • The class was divided into 2 groups:
    • Carthaginian (the non-boarder of Ateneo)
    • Roman (the boarder of Ateneo)
  • Rizal was placed at the bottom of the class and assigned with the Carthaginians.
  • Students competed for these positions throughout the empire by asking those in authority to respond to questions based on the lesson for the day.
    • Emperor: The best students
    • Tribune: The second best students
    • Decurion: The third best students
    • Centurion: The fourth best students
    • Standard Bearer: The fifth best students

First Year in Ateneo (1872 - 1873)

  • — Rizal was called an "externo” or a living out student during his days in Ateneo Municipal.
    • Fr. Jose Bech - First professor of Jose Rizal in Ateneo.
    • After a month, Rizal became an emperor of his empire.
  • — Rizal took private lessons in Sta. Isabel College during noon recesses to improve his Spanish language.
  • — Summer Vacation (1873)
    • — Rizal didn’t enjoy his summer because his mother was in prison, so Neneng (Saturnina) brougth him to tanawan.
    • — Jose Alberto (Teodora’s half-brother) frequently visited Calamba to consult Teodora for guidance. After learning of this, his wife (Teodora Formoso) began to think that Teodora and Jose Alberto were planning a sinister scheme against her.
    • — Jose Alberto and Teodora was accused of trying to poison Jose Alberto’s wife.

Second year in Ateneo (1873 - 1874)

  • — Rizal repented having neglected his studies the previous year simply
  • — To regain his lost class leadership Rizal studied harder
  • — At the end of the school year, Rizal received excellent grades in all subjects and a gold medal

Third year in Ateneo (1874 - 1875)

  • — June 16, 1875
    • — He became an interno in Ateneo.
    • — Fr. Francisco de Paula Sanchez, Fr. Francisco de Paula Sanchez, one of his professors, inspired him to study harder and write poetry. Rizal‘s best professor in Ateneo.

Fourth year in Ateneo (1875- 1876)

  • — March 23, 1877
    • — José Rizal, 16 years old, received from his Alma Mater, Ateneo Municipal, the degree of Bachelor of Arts, with highest honors.

    — He graduated as one of the nine students in his class declared sobresaliente or outstanding.

Rizal excelled both in academics and in the arts.

Rizal explored other forms of art, enrolling in the School of Fine Arts under Don Augustin Saez. He also tried his hand at sculpture, carving images of the Virgin Mary and the Sacred Heart of Jesus. He also sculpted busts of his favorite professors, who would later learn about the works and be pleased with them. Rizal also studied music and took singing lessons. (Guerrero, 2012)

  • Sculpture Works in Ateneo
    • — Rizal carved an image of The Virgin Mary on a piece of batikuling (Philippine hardwood) with his pocket knife.
    • — Father Lleonart (Jesuits Father) was amazed and request to Jose to carve for him an image of Sacred Heart of Jesus.
  • Extra-Curricular Activities in Ateneo
    • — Jose was an active member, late secretary of Marian Congregation a religious society. (Mentor: Fr. Pablo Pastells, SJ)
  • Poems Written in Ateneo
    • Mi Primera Inspiracion (My First Inspiration)
    • El Embarque: Himmo a la Flota de Magallanes (The Departure: Hymm to Magellan’s Fleet)
    • Y Es Espanol: Elcano, el Primero en dar la Vuelta al Mundo (And He is Spanish: Elcano, the First to Circumnavigate the World)
    • El Combante: Urbiztondo, Terror de Jolo (The Battle: Urbiztondo, Terror of Jolo)
    • In Memory of my Town - a tender poem in honor of Calamba
    • A Farewell Dialogue of the Students -the last poem written by Rizal in Ateneo.
  • Rizal’s Poem on Education
    • — In his poem "Through Education The Motherland Receives Light," he shares his thoughts on the importance of education.
    • — It is noticeable in "Intimidate Alliance Between Religion and Good Education" (Por la Educación Recibe Lustre la Patria) how important religion is to learning.
  • Rizal’s Religious Poems
    • Al Niño Jesus (To the Child Jesus)- written when he was 14 years old, expressing his Catholic faith devotion.
    • A La Virgen Maria (To The Virgin Mary)
    • — He excelled in all subjects in the surveying course in Ateneo.
    • March 23, 1877 - he received his degree of Bachelor of Arts with honors.

Education at University of Sto. Tomas (1877-1882)

April 1877- Rizal was 16 years old enrolled at the UST and took the course on Philosophy and Letters

  • — Rizal was still unsure of what course to take
  • — He pursued his studies in UST because the Bachelor of Arts course during Spanish times was equivalent only to the high school and junior college today.
  • — For his first term, Rizal studied at the Philosophy program.

Rizal Took Up the Medical Course

Rizal took up medicine, enrolling simultaneously in preparatory medical course and the regular first year medical course.

  • — Reasons Why He Studied Medicine
    • - To cure his mother’s falling eyesight
    • - Fr. Ramon wrote back advising Jose to take up medicine
    • - Highly in demand course Rizal entered a very different world in UST as a student

Rizal spent his time studying. He returned to Ateneo to take up a land surveying course which was then a vocational course

He completed the surveyor’s course and was awarded the title of “peritoagrimensor” (expert surveyor) with the grade of excellent

Although he received low marks in some areas, he received outstanding grades in the majority. Despite his primary interest in literature, he felt that he could be more useful as a physician given the options available to Filipinos at the time, which included law, medicine, and the priesthood.

Literary Works of Rizal in UST

Liceo Artistico Literario de Manila was an association of art enthusiasts in Manila regularly held literary contests that were open to Filipino Spaniards and the mestizos.

  • Year 1879
    • — Rizal submitted the poem “A La Juventud Filipina” (To The Filipino Youth) and won first prize. The poem has the often-quoted adage “Kabataan ang pag-asa ng bayan.” (“Bella esperanza de la Patria mia!”)
    • — Fr. Ramon wrote back advising Jose to take up medicine
  • Year 1880
    • El Consejo De Los Dioses (The Council of Gods)- Winning masterpiece submitted to Liceo, 2nd time; Price: gold ring bearing the engraved bust of Cervantes.
    • — He also wrote “Junto al Pasig” (Beside the Pasig) and “A Filipinas” (To the Philippines) while in the university.
    • AI M.R.P. Pablo Ramon, Recto del Ateneo, en Sus Dias - A poem to thank Father Pablo who had been kind and helpful to him.

Rizal decided to leave for Europe

He was determined to pursue his studies, so:

  • — Without his parents’ consent, but with the support of his brother Paciano and his uncle Antonio Rivera, Rizal decided to continue his studies in Spain.
  • — Rizal left Manila and traveled alone in Madrid on May 3, 1882.

Universidad Central de Madrid (Spain)

In Madrid, Rizal also studied philosophy and the letters at the Universidad Central de Madrid and attended classes at the Escuela de Bellas Artes de San Fernando, where he honed his skills in painting and sculpture.

Rizal completed medicine at the Universidad Central de Madrid in 1882,

He also studied in Paris and Heidelberg (Germany), where he pursued further studies in ophthalmology and took courses in various fields such as languages, culture, and history.

Rizal's Life Abroad

After his studies in the Philippines, Rizal traveled to Europe to pursue higher education and immerse himself in a broader intellectual environment.
