Lesson 1: Rizal's Life and Studies in Europe

Introduction

José Rizal's time in Europe was a period of significant intellectual growth, academic achievement, and political awakening. This lesson explores his educational journey and experiences in various European countries.

Arrival in Europe

Rizal's European journey began in 1882:

  • Departure from the Philippines (May 3, 1882)
  • Initial stop in Singapore
  • Journey through the Suez Canal
  • Arrival in Barcelona, Spain (June 16, 1882)

Studies in Madrid

At the Universidad Central de Madrid (1882-1885):

  • Licentiate in Medicine
    • Specialized in ophthalmology
    • Studied under prominent professors
  • Licentiate in Philosophy and Letters
    • Enhanced his literary skills
    • Developed critical thinking
  • Additional Studies
    • French, German, and English languages
    • Fencing and shooting
    • Art and sculpture

Specialized Training in Paris

At the University of Paris (1885-1886):

  • Advanced studies in ophthalmology
  • Training under Dr. Louis de Weckert
  • Research in eye diseases
  • Cultural exposure and artistic development

Studies in Heidelberg

At the University of Heidelberg (1886):

  • Further specialization in ophthalmology
  • Research in eye diseases
  • Publication of Noli Me Tángere
  • Cultural exchange with German scholars

Impact of European Education

Rizal's European education significantly influenced his development:

  • Professional growth as a physician
  • Intellectual development and critical thinking
  • Exposure to European liberalism
  • Formation of nationalist ideas
  • Development of literary and artistic skills
