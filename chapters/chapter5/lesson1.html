Lesson 1: Three Significant Articles

Introduction

This chapter explores three key works of Jose Rizal—The Philippines a Century Hence, The Indolence of the Filipinos, and To the Young Women of Malolos. These writings exposed the struggles of Filipinos under colonial rule and encouraged national awakening. It also examines how Rizal's ideas shaped the Filipino concepts of bayani and kabayanihan, and how he became a symbol of Philippine nationalism, inspiring the fight for freedom and dignity.

"The Philippines a Century Hence" (1889)

- Filipinas dentro de cien años (translated as The Philippines Within One Hundred Years or The Philippines a Century Hence) was an essay written by Dr. Jose Rizal. The article was published in La Solidaridad in Madrid. It was intended to predict the future of the country within 100 years. It reflected Rizal's views about the Philippines’ glorious past, the deterioration of its economy, and the sufferings of native Filipinos under Spanish rule.

- Rizal warned Spain about the catastrophic end of its domination in the essay. He reminded the colonizers that the circumstances that led to the French Revolution could also have a powerful effect on the Philippine islands. Rizal's objective in writing the essay was to promote a sense of nationalism among Filipinos and encourage them to fight for their rights.

  • Causes of Misery
    • As outlined in The Philippines a Century Hence: Summary and Analysis, Dr. Jose Rizal's essay began with an analysis of the causes of the miseries suffered by the Filipino people. These causes are listed as follows:
      • 1. Spain's Military Laws
      • – The implementation of military laws caused a significant decrease in the Philippine population. Militarization also led to widespread poverty and the abandonment of farmlands. The neglect of the family unit as a societal foundation resulted in the retardation of every aspect of Filipino life.
