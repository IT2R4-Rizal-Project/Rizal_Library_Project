Lesson 2: The Philippines in the 19th Century

Introduction

The 19th century was a period of significant transformation in the Philippines, marked by social, political, and economic changes that would ultimately shape the nation's path to independence. This era provides crucial context for understanding Rizal's life and works.

Social Structure and Conditions

The Philippine society during this period was characterized by a complex social hierarchy:

  • Spanish colonial administration and clergy
  • Local elite (principalia)
  • Middle class (illustrados)
  • Common people (indios)

Key Social Issues:

  • Racial discrimination and social inequality
  • Limited access to education for the masses
  • Religious influence on daily life
  • Emergence of the ilustrado class

Economic Developments

The 19th century saw significant economic changes in the Philippines:

  • Opening of the Suez Canal (1869)
  • Development of cash crop economy
  • Growth of international trade
  • Emergence of a money economy
  • Development of infrastructure and transportation

Political Climate

The political landscape was marked by:

  • Spanish colonial rule and administration
  • Emergence of reform movements
  • Growth of nationalist sentiments
  • Influence of liberal ideas from Europe
  • Rise of the Propaganda Movement

Cultural and Educational Landscape

This period saw significant developments in education and culture:

  • Establishment of modern educational institutions
  • Growth of the ilustrado class
  • Influence of European ideas and literature
  • Development of Filipino nationalism
  • Emergence of a distinct Filipino identity
